Article 171: Contract Formation
A contract is validly formed when there is a clear offer, acceptance, legal capacity, and lawful object. Electronic contracts are valid. Verbal contracts binding under QAR 10,000.

Article 172: Written Requirements
Contracts exceeding QAR 10,000 must be in writing. Real estate and agency agreements must be written. Electronic signatures valid.

Article 173: Good Faith Requirement
Parties must perform contracts in good faith. Bad faith damages available.

Article 174: Public Policy
Contracts violating public policy or morals are void.

Article 175: Capacity Requirements
Minors under 18 lack capacity. Corporate entities must have proper authorization.

Article 180: Interpretation Rules
Ambiguities construed against drafter. Commercial context considered.

Article 185: Contract Termination
Contracts may be terminated by: mutual consent, performance, expiration, material breach, or force majeure.

Article 186: Material Breach Definition
Failure that substantially deprives benefit. 30-day cure period reasonable.

Article 190: Limitation of Liability
Parties may limit liability but CANNOT exclude liability for: fraud, gross negligence, personal injury, or public policy violations.

Article 191: Liquidated Damages
Must be reasonable estimate of actual damages. Penalties unenforceable.

Article 195: Governing Law
Parties may choose governing law. If not specified, Qatar law applies. Mandatory Qatar provisions cannot be excluded.

Article 200: Confidentiality
Parties must maintain confidentiality of proprietary information. Obligations survive termination for 3 years minimum.

Article 205: Assignment and Delegation
Rights assignable unless personal. Duties delegable with consent.

Article 210: Intellectual Property
Unless specified otherwise, IP rights belong to creator. Work-for-hire must be explicit. Clients should own deliverables they pay for.

Article 215: Warranties
Implied warranty of merchantability. Fitness for purpose implied if specified.

Article 220: Payment Terms
Payment terms must be clear. Late payment interest maximum 9% annually. Payment cannot be unreasonably withheld.

Article 225: Set-off Rights
Mutual debts may be set off. Notice required.

Article 230: Force Majeure
Force majeure events excuse performance. Party must give prompt notice and mitigate impact.

Article 235: Notice Provisions
Written notice required. Deemed received 3 business days after mailing.

Article 240: Severability
Invalid provisions severable. Remainder enforceable.

Article 245: Entire Agreement
Contract supersedes prior agreements. Modifications must be written.

Article 250: Third Party Rights
No third party benefits unless specified.
